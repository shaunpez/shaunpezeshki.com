# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Visitors are potential consulting clients, collaborators, friends, readers, and people trying to understand Shaun Pezeshki beyond a single professional label. They may arrive through search, social links, a referral, or a specific article. They want to quickly understand what Shaun does, how he thinks, what he writes about, and whether his mix of technical skill, business judgment, and personal depth feels trustworthy.

## Product Purpose

This site is Shaun's personal brand home: part consultant profile, part writing archive, part proof of lived perspective. It should present Shaun as a capable technical strategist and business operator while also making room for his softer, reflective personal voice. Success means visitors can navigate easily, understand the consulting value, and feel invited to read enough of the writing to understand the depth of the person behind the work.

## Brand Personality

Warm, personal, technical. The tone should feel candid, thoughtful, approachable, and clear. The site should make people want to learn about Shaun as a whole person: someone who can solve business and technology problems, but who also writes with emotional honesty about identity, community, growth, and modern life.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic SaaS landing pages, corporate consultant templates, dark "tech bro" aesthetics, overly polished agency gloss, beige newsletter minimalism, and anything that makes the personal writing feel like an afterthought. The site should not feel cold, performative, sales-heavy, or visually interchangeable with a startup homepage.

## Design Principles

- Lead with the whole person: consulting credibility and personal writing should support each other instead of competing.
- Keep navigation obvious: readers and potential clients should always know where to go next.
- Make the site bright and human: favor warmth, daylight, and approachable details over dark technical drama.
- Let content carry depth: preserve the existing writing and page content while making it easier to scan, enter, and continue reading.
- Use restraint with playfulness: small memorable moments are welcome, but the experience should stay clean, simple, and readable.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast and keyboard accessibility. Keep body text comfortable for long-form reading, avoid motion that cannot be reduced, and do not rely on color alone for meaning. The design should feel inclusive and readable for a broad audience across age, device size, and familiarity with Shaun's work.
